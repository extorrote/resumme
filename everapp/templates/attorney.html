What are the penalties for a DUI conviction in Tennessee?

    Penalties for DUI in Tennessee include:


    1st. Infringement:
  • Jail time: 48 hours-11 months and 29 days (BAC of 0.20 or greater minimum jail time 7 consecutive days)
  • License revocation: 1 year,
  • You will be ordered to participate in a DUI school,
  • For compensation to any person who has suffered physical injury or personal loss,
  • Fine of $350-$1,500,
  • For your first violation, the average costs will rise to $4,900 for tow truck, bail, high risk insurance, court costs, school, and compensation,
  • The judge may order you to install an ignition interlock device in your vehicle at your expense, minimum 1st cost. year $810, and
  • At the judge's discretion, he or she may order alcohol and drug treatment.
    2nd. Infringement:
  • Jail time: 40 days to 29 days,
  • Mandatory fine of $600 to $3,500
  • License revocation for 2 years. Restrictive license available after 1 year,
  • Subject to vehicle seizure or confiscation
  • You will be ordered to attend DUI school,
  • The judge may order you to install an ignition interlock device in your vehicle at your expense for 6 months after reinstallation.
  • If there are two (2) DUI convictions within 5 years, an ignition interlock device will be required at your expense after reinstatement.y
  • Will pay compensation to any person for suffering or loss
    3rd. Infringement:
  • Jail time: 120 days to 11 months and 29 days in jail,
  • Mandatory fines of $1,100 to $10,000
  • License revocation for a period of 3-10 years, WITHOUT the possibility of obtaining a restricted license.
  • Subject to vehicle seizure or confiscation
  • DUI school,
  • The judge may order you to install an ignition interlock device in your vehicle at your expense, and
  • If there are two (2) DUI convictions within 5 years, an ignition interlock device will be required at your expense after reinstatement.
    4a. Infringement:
  • Class E Felony,
  • Jail time: 1 year (365 days) with a minimum of 150 consecutive days served,
  • Mandatory fine of $3,000 to $15,000,
  • License revocation for a period of 5 years, NO restricted license available,
  • Subject to vehicle seizure or confiscation
  • DUI school,
  • The judge may order the installation of an ignition interlock device at his expense,
  • If there are two (2) DUI convictions within 5 years, an ignition interlock device will be required, at your expense, upon reinstatement.

Springfield Courthouse

Located in 529 SHOUTH BROWN STREET
Robertson County Courthouse in Springfield, Tennessee dates from 1879.[1] The building's north wing, south wing, and central clock tower were added in 1929–30. The architect was W.C. Smith and the building contractor was the firm of Patton & McInturff.[2] The architects for the expansion were Edward E. Dougherty and Thomas W. Gardner of Nashville.[3]

The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.[1][3]
